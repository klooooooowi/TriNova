Our Research

★ Our study investigates how different home environments influence students’ academic performance. Key areas of focus:

☆ Parenting Styles: How parental guidance affects study habits.

☆ Emotional Support: The role of encouragement and care in student motivation.

☆ Household Responsibilities: Tasks or duties at home that impact school focus.

☆ Physical Study Environment: How lighting, noise, and organization affect learning.

☆ Comparative Analysis: Differences in how Grade 7 (early adolescents) and Grade 12 (late adolescents) respond to their home environment.

Research Objectives

☆ Examine how parenting styles affect students’ study habits and academic outcomes.

☆ Investigate how students manage challenges at home and school.

☆ Identify household responsibilities or experiences that impact students’ ability to focus on schoolwork.

☆ Compare the effects of home environment factors on early adolescents (Grade 7) versus late adolescents (Grade 12).

☆ Provide recommendations that help students, parents, and educators improve learning and academic performance.

Why This Matters

★ Understanding the home environment’s impact on learning helps:

☆ Students: Gain awareness of their study habits and how their home life affects them.

☆ Parents: Learn how to provide better support for their children.

☆ Teachers/Educators: Develop strategies that complement students’ home environments.

Research Information

☆ Title: Grade 7 vs. Grade 12: The Different Types of Home Environment That Affect the Academic Performance of Learners in APEC Schools Marikina Heights

☆ School Year: 2025–2026

☆ Participants:

★ Grade 7 students (early adolescence, approx. ages 12–14)

★ Grade 12 students (late adolescence, approx. ages 17–18)
